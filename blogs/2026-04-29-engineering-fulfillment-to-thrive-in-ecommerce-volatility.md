E-commerce operates in an increasingly turbulent environment. As Perry notes, "Unpredictable tariffs, high interest rates, growing inflation, and rising labor and transportation costs are squeezing brands from every direction."

The impact of operational failures carries significant weight. Research indicates that "shoppers tell an average of 16 people about a bad delivery interaction, compared to just 9 for a positive one," underscoring why fulfillment excellence matters competitively.

Perry identifies a core problem: many brands rely on disconnected systems and manual processes that lack resilience. When errors occur—such as picking mistakes or inventory mismatches—the financial consequences compound. She argues that "operational agility is no longer optional, it's a differentiator."

## Five Strategies for Operational Resilience

1. **Quality as foundational architecture** — Changes undergo controlled pilots with validation checkpoints before scaling.

2. **Disciplined change management** — All adjustments receive documentation, testing, and review before implementation.

3. **Unified data systems** — Stord One Commerce integrates 100+ systems to create a single operational source of truth.

4. **Cross-functional alignment** — Shared visibility reduces silos and coordinates organizational response.

5. **Training and enablement** — Structured onboarding ensures consistent execution of new processes.

Perry concludes that successful brands aren't simply faster reactors; they build "operations capable of adapting quickly and consistently, without sacrificing accuracy or the customer experience."
