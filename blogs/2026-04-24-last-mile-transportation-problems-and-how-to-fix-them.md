Your shipping budget represents one of the most challenging expenses to control on a profit-and-loss statement. More than half of total shipping costs stem from a single stage: the last-mile delivery—the concluding journey from a local warehouse to a customer's residence. Unlike B2B freight operations moving pallets between predictable locations, this final leg involves dozens of unique stops, unpredictable traffic, and heightened delay risks.

The last mile represents your final opportunity to create a positive impression. Research indicates that "85% of consumers say they will never shop with a brand again after a single poor delivery experience."

## Four Core Problems Breaking Last-Mile Delivery

**Managing High Delivery Costs**
Last-mile delivery accounts for the highest expense in fulfillment, consuming "53% of total shipping spend." Shipping typically consumes "10-15% of a brand's total revenue," with this percentage continuing to rise.

**Single-Carrier Dependency**
When Pitney Bowes filed for bankruptcy in August 2024, brands relying on them for final-mile delivery experienced "3 to 6 week disruptions and cost increases of 25% or more."

**Failed Deliveries and Theft**
Requiring a second delivery attempt doubles shipment costs. Additionally, "41% of Americans have experienced porch piracy."

**Demand for Faster Shipping**
Customers now expect two-day delivery as standard. Statistics show "21% of shoppers abandon their cart due to slow shipping, and 39% abandon because of shipping costs entirely."

## Solutions for Last-Mile Success

**Intelligent Carrier Selection** — Optimal routes incorporate zip-code-to-zip-code performance history, real-time carrier capacity, order characteristics, and service level equivalency.

**Distributed Inventory** — Placing inventory in fulfillment centers closer to demand concentrations enables two-day ground shipping without air freight premiums.

**Proactive Communication** — Customers receiving shipping updates reduce support inquiries. At approximately "$5 per WISMO interaction," proactive communication prevents significant recurring costs.

**Data-Driven Delivery Promises** — "58% of consumers want to see an exact delivery date before they complete a purchase. Only 1% of brands provide one."

**Connected Systems** — When OMS, WMS, and carrier networks integrate seamlessly, information flows uninterrupted from checkout to doorstep.

## Conclusion

Only "34% of brands delivered as promised" in Stord's 2025 Mystery Shopping study, with "14% missing their own commitments by an average of seven days." The winning brands of the coming years will construct consumer experiences that earn trust with every order.
